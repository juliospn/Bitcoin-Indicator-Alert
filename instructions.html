Basic Instructions: This is a long-term strategy.

Approximately every four years, the halving occurs, causing a shock to the supply, which leads to a bull run and overbought prices. After this, a bear market and oversold prices follow, and then a period of lateralization until the next halving occurs.

This is the Bitcoin price cycle.

Use NUPL combined with the time-cycle to determine whether the price is in an overbought or oversold region. Then, use the funding rate to determine in the short-term whether the price is in an overbought or oversold region.

You can check this strategy better seeing the past time grapichs of each indicators.

- Time Cycles:

1º year: Bullrun

2º year: BearMarket

3º year: Lateralization

4º year: Lateralization

- NUPL (long-term indicator):

Very Overbought: 0.75 ~ 0.99

Overbought: 0.50 ~ 0.75

Neutral: 0.25 ~ 0.50

Oversold: 0.00 ~ 0.25

Very oversold: -0.50 ~ 0.00

- Funding Rate (short-term indicator):

OverBought Region: ++ 0.01

Long traders pay FR to short traders: 0.00 ~ +0.01

Short traders pay FR to long traders: -0.01 ~ 0.00

OverSold Region: -- 0.01
